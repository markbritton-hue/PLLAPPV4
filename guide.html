App Guide

An overview of every page in PowerLift Live and who it's for

Getting Started
Setup
For: Coaches, Athletes, Judges, Announcers

The first page every user should visit. Select your meet from the list of active events, choose your school from the dropdown, and enter your athlete's name — then save your configuration. Once saved, all other pages will automatically load data for your meet and your athlete. You only need to do this once per device — settings are remembered.

Go to Setup
Pages
Team Athletes
For: Coaches, Spectators

Shows all athletes from your school competing in the meet, grouped by weight class. View each athlete's attempt card, current totals, and placement. Useful for coaches and spectators following their team throughout the day.

Go to Team Athletes
Platform View
For: Judges, Announcers, Meet Officials

Shows the current lifter on the platform in real time — including their name, school, current attempt, weight on the bar, and lift decisions. Designed to be displayed on a screen at the platform for judges and announcers to follow along. Updates automatically as the meet progresses.

Go to Platform View
Athlete View
For: Individual Athletes, Parents

A personal view for a single athlete. Shows their full attempt card — all three lifts with weights, good lift indicators, and running total. Also shows the weight difference to the leader for each lift, so athletes and coaches can see exactly how much they need to close the gap. Perfect for athletes warming up in the back room or parents following along from the stands.

Go to Athlete View
Coaches View
For: Coaches

A coach-focused dashboard showing all athletes from your school on a single screen. See every lifter's current attempts, totals, and status at a glance. Designed to help coaches manage their team throughout the meet without jumping between pages.

Go to Coaches View
Team Standings
For: Coaches, Athletes

Shows the full roster and results for your school's team. Coaches and athletes can see how every member of their team is performing, including individual lift totals and placement within their weight class.

Go to Team Standings
All Results
For: Everyone

Displays the final or current standings for the meet. Shows all lifters ranked by total, with their squat, bench, and deadlift results. Great for spectators and coaches to follow how competitors are placing throughout the day.

Go to All Results
State Record Attempts
For: Officials, Announcers

Highlights any lifters who are attempting a state record during the meet. Shows the lifter's name, school, lift type, and the weight being attempted. Allows officials and announcers to easily track and announce record attempts as they happen.

Go to State Records
Resources
For: Everyone

A collection of helpful links and documents for competitors, coaches, and families. Includes:

  • Rules — Official competition rules and regulations for Michigan High School Powerlifting
  • MHSPLA Website — Official site for news, events, and standings
  • State Meet Judging — Judging criteria and guidelines for the state meet
  • State Records — Michigan state records for all weight classes and divisions
  • State Record Attempts — Live view of athletes attempting to break a state record in the current meet
  • Director Portal — Add and manage meets for the event picker on the setup page
