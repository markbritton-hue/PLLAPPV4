# PowerLift Live — User & Coach Guide

> **Disclaimer:** PowerLift Live (PLL App) is an unofficial, independent tool and is **not the official scoring app** for any competition. It is **not affiliated with, endorsed by, or supported by the Michigan High School Powerlifting Association (MHSPL)**. For official results, always refer to the meet director or official MHSPL resources.

---

**PowerLift Live** is a free web app that lets coaches, athletes, and families follow a powerlifting meet in real time — right from their phone or any browser. No app download needed.

---

## How to Open the App

Open your browser and go to the URL your meet coordinator shared (it will look something like `https://yourschool.github.io/PLLApp`). Bookmark it so you can get back to it quickly.

That's it. No login, no account, no download.

---

## First-Time Setup

The first time you open the app, a setup prompt will appear. You'll need to connect a Google Sheet before any data will load. Go to **Setup** and either:
- Paste the Google Sheet URL for your meet, or
- Select your meet from the **Upcoming Meets** list (if your meet has been registered)

In Setup you can also configure:
- **School Name** — used to highlight your school's athletes throughout the app
- **Athlete Name** *(optional)* — enables the My Athlete panel in Athlete View for tracking one specific athlete's rank and status
- **Announcements** — toggle on/off (voice announcements on Coaches View cover all your school's athletes regardless of this setting)
- **Auto-Refresh Interval** — how often the app refreshes data (default: 20 seconds)

Settings are saved automatically and persist if you close and reopen the app.

## For Families & Fans

### What can I see?

Once the meet is running, tap **All Results** from the home screen. You'll see every athlete in the meet organized by weight class with their place ranking and total weight lifted.

Your school's athletes will be **highlighted in green** so they're easy to find.

### What do the numbers mean?

Each athlete has three lifts: **Squat**, **Bench Press**, and **Deadlift**. They get three attempts at each. The best successful attempt from each lift adds up to their **Total**.

- **Green attempt** = good lift ✓
- **Red attempt** = failed lift ✗
- **Total** = their score for the meet

Athletes are ranked within their weight class by total — highest total wins.

### Can I see my athlete's attempts as they happen?

Yes — tap **All Results** and turn on **Auto-Refresh**. The page will update automatically so you always see the latest numbers.

## For Coaches

As a coach, you have access to several views depending on what you need.

---

### Coaches View — Your Most Important Tool

**Coaches View** is designed specifically for coaches on the meet floor. It shows **only your athletes** sorted by their current position in the lifting order across all platforms.

Each athlete card shows:
- Which **platform** they're on
- Their **position** in the lifting order (Lifting, On Deck, Position 3, etc.)
- Their current **attempt weight**
- Their squat, bench, and deadlift progress

Tap the **down arrow** on a card to expand it and see full attempt details. Tap the **platform button** on a card to jump directly to that platform's view.

**How to use it on meet day:**
1. Open the app and tap **Coaches View**
2. Tap **Auto-Refresh: OFF** to turn auto-refresh on (button turns green)
3. Tap the **speaker icon** to enable voice announcements
4. Keep the screen on — it will update automatically

> The app remembers whether auto-refresh was on or off, so if you close the tab and reopen it, it picks up where it left off.

#### Voice Announcements

Tap the **speaker button** on the Coaches View page to activate voice alerts. The app will announce position changes for **all of your school's athletes** — no specific athlete name required. Announcements include:
- **On Deck** — the athlete is next to lift
- **Lifting** — the athlete is currently on the platform
- **Done** — the attempt result (good lift, failed, or new state record)

> On iOS, you must tap the speaker button once to unlock audio for that session.

---

### Athlete View — Compare Within a Weight Class

**Athlete View** lets you select any weight class and see all athletes ranked side by side with their attempts and totals.

- **Rank badges** highlight 1st (gold), 2nd (silver), and 3rd (bronze) place
- If you've entered an athlete name in Setup, a **My Athlete panel** appears at the top showing that athlete's current rank and lifting status
- Useful for tracking how your athlete compares to the field during the meet

---

### Team Athletes — Full Roster View

Tap **Team Athletes** to see your entire varsity and JV roster organized by weight class. Each athlete's card shows:
- Body weight
- All three attempts per lift as they happen
- Running total
- Which platform they're assigned to (shown as a purple badge)
- V or JV designation badge

This is useful for reviewing your team's performance after a flight or at the end of the meet.

---

### Platform View — Watch a Single Platform

If you're standing at a specific platform, tap **Platform View** and select that platform number. You'll see the full lifting order for that platform — who just lifted, who's up now, and who's next.

A **"Just Finished"** indicator appears at the top when the most recent athlete completes their lift, staying visible until the next athlete finishes.

---

### Team Standings — Where Does Your School Rank?

Tap **Team Standings** to see how all the schools are stacking up in total team points. Your school is shown along with all other competing schools, sorted by total points.

Turn on auto-refresh here too if you want it to update throughout the meet.

---

### All Results — Full Meet Results

Tap **All Results** for a complete view of every athlete in the meet, grouped by weight class. Athletes are ranked within their class (1st, 2nd, 3rd, etc.) by their total weight lifted.

Your school's athletes are highlighted in green so they stand out.

## Resources

The **Resources** page links to:
- **Official MHSPLA rules** document
- **MHSPLA website** — news, events, and standings
- **State Meet Judging** criteria
- **State Records** on the MHSPLA website
- **State Record Attempts** — live view of athletes in the current meet who are attempting to break a Michigan state record (see below)

---

### State Record Attempts

The **State Record Attempts** page (found under Resources) shows:
- Athletes in the current meet whose attempt weight would break the Michigan state record
- Which platform they're on and their current position in the lifting order
- How far above the current record their attempt is
- A running log of state records that have been **beaten** or **failed** at this meet

---

## Quick Tips for Meet Day

- **Coaches View + Auto-Refresh on** = your phone becomes a real-time tracker for all your athletes at once
- **Voice announcements** are the fastest way to know when your athlete is stepping up — set your athlete's name in Setup and tap the speaker icon on Coaches View
- If the page stops updating, check your phone's wifi or cell signal — the app needs internet to refresh data
- All pages work on phones, tablets, and laptops
- You can have multiple pages open in different browser tabs — for example, Coaches View on one tab and Team Standings on another
- The app does not require a login and works for anyone who has the link — share it freely with parents and athletes

---

## Something Looks Wrong?

If data looks outdated or missing, try:
1. Tapping the **Auto-Refresh** button to force a manual refresh
2. Reloading the page
3. Asking the meet coordinator to check that the Google Sheet is still shared and being updated by the scoring software

If the home screen shows a **"No Active Meets At This Time"** banner, the live results server is offline — check back on meet day.
